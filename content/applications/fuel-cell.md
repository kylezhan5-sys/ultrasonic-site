## Overview

PEM (Proton Exchange Membrane) fuel cells require a precisely deposited catalyst layer on the membrane electrode assembly (MEA). The catalyst layer — typically platinum nanoparticles on carbon support — must be uniform, thin, and free of defects to maximize electrochemical performance.

Ultrasonic spray coating is the preferred deposition method for research, pilot, and production-scale MEA fabrication because it delivers:

- **High material utilization** (90–95%) for expensive Pt catalysts
- **Uniform droplet size** (10–50 μm) for consistent layer thickness
- **Gentle atomization** that preserves catalyst nanoparticle structure
- **Programmable deposition** for complex multi-layer MEA architectures
