Ultrasonic Spray Coating for Solar Cell Applications

Next-generation solar cell technologies including perovskite, CIGS, and organic photovoltaics require precise, uniform thin-film deposition of functional layers. Ultrasonic spray coating provides the film quality, thickness control, and material efficiency needed for both R&D and production-scale solar cell manufacturing.

Solar Cell Coating Applications

  • Perovskite Solar Cells: Perovskite absorber layer, electron transport layer (TiO2, SnO2), hole transport layer (Spiro-OMeTAD, PEDOT:PSS)
  • CIGS Solar Cells: CdS buffer layer, ZnO window layer, anti-reflection coating
  • Silicon Solar Cells: Anti-reflection coating (SiNx, MgF2), passivation layers
  • Organic Photovoltaics: Active layer, charge transport layers, encapsulation

Advantages for Solar Cell Manufacturing

Ultrasonic spray coating enables large-area, uniform deposition of functional layers with precise thickness control (±5% across 200x200 mm). The gentle atomization process preserves the crystalline structure of perovskite precursors and the morphology of organic semiconductor films, resulting in higher device efficiency compared to spin coating or doctor blade methods for large-area substrates.
