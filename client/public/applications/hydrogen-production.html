Ultrasonic Equipment for Hydrogen Production

Green hydrogen production via PEM water electrolysis requires high-performance electrode coatings that combine catalytic activity, durability, and cost efficiency. Ultrasonic spray coating enables precise deposition of iridium and platinum catalysts, reducing precious metal loading while maintaining or improving electrolyzer performance.

PEM Electrolyzer Catalyst Coating

The performance and cost of PEM electrolyzers are largely determined by the catalyst loading and distribution on the membrane electrode assembly (MEA). Ultrasonic spray coating achieves:

  • IrO2 anode loading as low as 0.5 mg/cm2 with maintained performance
  • Pt cathode loading of 0.1–0.3 mg/cm2
  • Uniform coating on rough titanium PTL (porous transport layer) surfaces
  • Compatible with aqueous and alcohol-based catalyst inks

Sonochemical Hydrogen Production

Ultrasonic sonochemistry can also be applied directly to water splitting reactions, where acoustic cavitation generates hydroxyl radicals and reactive species that facilitate hydrogen evolution. This emerging application is being explored for decentralized, low-cost hydrogen production.
