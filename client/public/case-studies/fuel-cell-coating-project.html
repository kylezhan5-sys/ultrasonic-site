PEM Fuel Cell Catalyst Coating

Customer Background

A leading European PEM fuel cell stack manufacturer was scaling up MEA production from pilot to commercial scale. Their existing air-spray coating process was producing unacceptable coating non-uniformity (±15%) and wasting 50-60% of expensive platinum catalyst as overspray.

Challenge

The customer needed to:

  • Achieve coating uniformity of ±5% or better across 200x200 mm active area
  • Reduce platinum catalyst waste from 50-60% to below 10%
  • Scale from 5 MEAs/day to 50 MEAs/day without increasing floor space
  • Maintain compatibility with their existing Nafion/IPA/water catalyst ink formulation

SonoTech Solution

SonoTech supplied a custom-configured ultrasonic spray coating system with a 60 kHz nozzle (producing 25-35 um droplets), a 300x300 mm heated substrate platen (80°C), and a 4-axis motion system with 0.02 mm positioning accuracy. The system was programmed with a bi-directional raster scan pattern optimized for the customer's MEA size and catalyst loading target.

The catalyst ink (Pt/C 40% + Nafion 5% in IPA/water 1:1) was delivered by a precision syringe pump at 2 mL/min flow rate. Eight coating passes were used to build up the target loading of 0.3 mg Pt/cm2.

Results

Material Utilization

The customer achieved coating uniformity of ±2% (vs. ±15% previously), reduced platinum waste from 55% to 5%, and increased throughput to 60 MEAs/day using a single system. The improved coating quality also resulted in a 5% improvement in fuel cell stack power density.
