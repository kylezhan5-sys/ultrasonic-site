PEM Electrolyzer Electrode Coating

Customer Background

A green hydrogen startup was developing PEM water electrolyzers for industrial hydrogen production. Their primary challenge was the high cost of iridium oxide (IrO2) catalyst for the oxygen evolution reaction (OER) anode, which accounted for 30% of the total electrolyzer stack cost.

Challenge

The customer was using a manual brush coating method that resulted in non-uniform IrO2 distribution, high catalyst waste (40-50%), and inconsistent electrode performance between batches. They needed a scalable, reproducible coating process that could reduce IrO2 loading while maintaining electrolyzer performance.

SonoTech Solution

SonoTech provided an ultrasonic spray coating system configured for aqueous IrO2 catalyst ink (IrO2 nanoparticles in isopropanol/water with Nafion binder). The system used a 48 kHz nozzle to produce 30-40 um droplets, deposited onto titanium PTL substrates at room temperature.
