Li-Ion Battery Electrode Slurry Dispersion

Customer Background

A lithium-ion battery research institute was developing high-energy-density cathode materials (NMC811) and needed a faster, cleaner method for electrode slurry preparation compared to their existing planetary ball mill process.

Challenge

Ball milling required 4-6 hours to achieve adequate NMC811 and carbon black dispersion, introduced metallic contamination from grinding media, and could not be easily scaled for continuous production. The institute needed a process that could achieve D50 below 1 um in under 2 hours with no contamination.

SonoTech Solution

SonoTech supplied a 1000W ultrasonic homogenizer with a flow-through reactor cell for continuous processing. The NMC811/carbon black/PVDF slurry in NMP was processed at 70% amplitude with 5s on / 2s off pulse mode to prevent overheating. Multiple passes through the flow cell achieved the target particle size distribution.
