Ultrasonic Horn (Sonotrode)

SonoTech ultrasonic horns amplify and focus ultrasonic vibration from transducers. Custom designs in titanium, steel, and aluminum for welding, cutting, and processing.

Product Overview

The SonoTech Ultrasonic Horn (also called a sonotrode or booster) is a precision-machined acoustic component that amplifies and transmits ultrasonic vibration from the transducer to the work surface. The horn geometry is carefully designed using finite element analysis (FEA) to achieve the desired amplitude amplification ratio while maintaining uniform vibration at the working face.

Frequently Asked Questions

Titanium alloy (Ti-6Al-4V) is the most common choice for high-power applications due to its excellent acoustic properties, fatigue resistance, and corrosion resistance. Aluminum 7075 is used for large-area welding horns where weight reduction is important. Tool steel is used for abrasive applications where wear resistance is critical.

The horn is machined to a length that is a half-wavelength at the operating frequency. After initial machining, the resonant frequency is measured and fine-tuned by removing small amounts of material from the non-critical end. SonoTech guarantees frequency accuracy within +/-50 Hz of the specified frequency.
