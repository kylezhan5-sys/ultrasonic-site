Ultrasonic Spray Coating System

Precision thin-film deposition with uniform droplet distribution. Engineered for fuel cell MEA, solar cell, battery electrode, and advanced material coating applications.

Product Overview

The SonoTech Ultrasonic Spray Coating System utilizes high-frequency ultrasonic vibration to atomize liquids into a fine, uniform mist. Unlike conventional air-spray systems, ultrasonic atomization produces a narrow droplet size distribution without requiring pressurized air, resulting in significantly higher material utilization and more uniform coating thickness.

The system is designed for precision thin-film applications where coating uniformity, material efficiency, and process repeatability are critical. It is widely used in fuel cell membrane electrode assembly (MEA) fabrication, solar cell anti-reflection coating, battery electrode preparation, and medical device surface treatment.

Working Principle

Ultrasonic spray coating works by applying high-frequency electrical energy to a piezoelectric transducer, which converts it into mechanical vibration. This vibration is transmitted to the nozzle tip, where it creates standing waves on the liquid surface. When the vibration amplitude exceeds a critical threshold, the liquid surface breaks up into a fine mist of uniformly sized droplets — a process known as ultrasonic atomization.

The droplet size is primarily controlled by the nozzle frequency: higher frequencies produce smaller droplets. The flow rate is controlled independently by a precision syringe pump or peristaltic pump, allowing complete decoupling of droplet size and flow rate.

Typical Applications

Fuel Cell MEA: Catalyst layer (Pt/C ink) coating on Nafion membrane or gas diffusion layer for PEM fuel cells.

Electrolyzer Electrodes: IrO₂ and RuO₂ catalyst coating on titanium substrates for PEM water electrolyzers.

Solar Cells: Anti-reflection coating, perovskite layer deposition, and PEDOT:PSS hole transport layer.

Battery Electrodes: Conductive carbon coating on current collectors, solid electrolyte thin films.

Medical Devices: Drug-eluting stent coating, implant surface functionalization, biosensor fabrication.

Optical Coatings: Anti-fog, hydrophobic, and photocatalytic TiO₂ coatings on glass and polymer substrates.
